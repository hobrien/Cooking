# Megruli Khachapuri

Adapted from [Helen Rennie](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sOTXIpMyiYI).

Combine in a large bowl:

- 230 g plain flour
- 1/2 tsp yeast
- 1 tsp sugar
- 1 tsp salt

Combine in a measuring cup and heat to 27°C:

- 85 g whole milk
- 85 g water
- 20 g extra virgin olive oil

Stir liquid ingredients into flour mixture. Mix until fully combined.
Let rest for 30 minutes.

Cover fingers with vegetable oil and do 12 stretch and folds, then
rest 30 minutes to 1 hour.

Split dough in half and form into two balls. Place each in a small
bowl with:

- 1 tbsp vegetable oil

Place bowls in fridge overnight or up to 3 days.

Remove dough from fridge 2 hours before baking and prepare filling.
Mix together:

- 150 g low-moisture whole milk mozzarella, grated
- 100 g sheep or goat feta
- 80 g whipped (or regular) cream cheese
- 1 egg yolk
- black pepper

Pour 1 tsp of vegetable oil on parchment paper and spread in a 20 cm
circle. Invert one of the bowls of dough over the parchment and wait
for the dough to drop. Flatten dough with your fingers into an 18 cm
circle, leaving it thicker in the middle. Add a ball of filling and
flatten it into a thick disk. Pull edges of dough and fold over the
filling. Press down to flatten into a 20 cm circle, leaving the edges
slightly puffier than the centre.

Brush with egg wash:

- 1 egg yolk
- 15 g sour cream

Cut a 1 cm hole in the middle of the pie. Sprinkle with:

- 50 g low-moisture whole milk mozzarella, grated

Bake in a 230°C oven for 12 minutes until golden brown.

## Notes

- Dough is very high hydration. It will be difficult to handle at
  first.
- Can add 1–2 tbsp of diced herbs to filling, though this is not
  traditional.
- Traditionally made with sulguni and imeruli cheese.
