# Baguettes

Adapted from Tartine Bread.

Prepare starter the night before. Add to container with residual
starter and stir:

- 50 g water
- 25 g bread flour
- 25 g whole wheat bread flour

Prepare poolish the night before. Combine in stand mixer bowl and
cover with plate:

- 50 g plain flour
- 50 g water
- 1/8 tsp yeast

In the morning add to stand mixer bowl with poolish:

- 100 g starter (50 g water)
- 125 g water
- 162.5 g AP flour
- 87.5 g bread flour

Mix until combined, then autolyse for 30 minutes to 1 hour.

Add before kneading:

- 6 g salt

Laminate dough and place in baking dish. Bulk ferment 3–12 hours
depending on temperature, doing 2–4 coil folds in the first few hours.

Tip dough onto counter, divide, and form into two tight balls. Rest
for 15 minutes.

Lightly flour tops of balls and flip over. Stretch into a 15 cm x
10 cm rectangle and roll into a log. Flip and build tension by pulling
log back and forth with bench scraper. Add more flour, then flip and
roll out to width of peel (35 cm). Dust couche with rice flour and
place breads 5 cm apart, tops down. Push breads together so that
couche folds up between them, add more rice flour, then tightly roll
up couche so the sides are supported. Proof for 2–3 hours.

Transfer breads to peel, score, and place on baking steel in a 250°C
oven. Cover with baking pan or add boiling water to bottom of oven.
Bake 15–20 minutes, uncovered after the first 10 minutes.
