# Hot Cross Buns

Adapted from the Foster Market cookbook

Mix in a small bowl and let site 5-10 min until foaming:

- 1/4 cup warm (45C) water
- 1/2 tsp sugar
- 1 tsp yeast

Mix in a medium bowl
- 55g melted butter
- 1 cup warm (45C) milk
- 50 g sugar
- 1/2 tsp salt

Combine yeast with other liquid ingredients, then mix with

- 390 g AP flour

Knead until smooth and stretchy, form into a ball, and rest until doubled in size (45 - 60 min)

Place in an air-tight container in fridge overnight

Soak overnight in flavourable liquid of your choice:

- 1/3 cup currants or dark raisins
- 1/3 cup golden raisins
- 1/3 cup dried cranberries 

Drain and mix with:

- 1 tsp cinnamon
- 1/2 tsp mace
- 1/2 tsp nutmeg
- 1/2 tsp allspice
- grated zest of 1 orange
- grated zest of one lemon

Remove dough from fridge and allow to come up to room temp for 30 min

Knead fruit and spices into dough. Form into tight ball. Split in quarters and form into balls, split each ball into thirds and form into small a total of 12 buns. Place on sheet tray lined with baking paper and allow to proof for 1 hour

Prepare egg wash and brush on buns:

- 1 egg
- 2 tbps milk

Bake in a 350F oven for 25-30 min

Dip in Lemon glaze:
- 1/2 cup icing sugar
- 1 tbsp lemon juice
- grated zest of 1 lemon

Mix up fondent icing and pipe crosses:
- 50 g  fondant icing sugar
- 2 tsp water

Notes:

- I don't know if I mis-measured the flour, but I had to add an extra 90 g to get a workable dough
- I couldn't find golden raisins so I used sultanas
- I soaked the fruit in left over pomegranate juice
- lemon glaze is a bit thick. Use twice as much lemon juice next time
- I think I accidentally added a tbsp of water to the fondant. I added probably another 50 g of icing sugar and ended up with a think but pipeable icing that worked perfectly for the crosses
