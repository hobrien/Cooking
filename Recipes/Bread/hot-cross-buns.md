# Hot Cross Buns

Adapted from the Foster Market cookbook.

Mix in a small bowl and let sit 5-10 minutes until foaming:

- 1/4 cup warm (45C) water
- 1/2 tsp sugar
- 1 tsp yeast

Mix in a medium bowl:

- 55 g melted butter
- 1 cup warm (45C) milk
- 50 g sugar
- 1/2 tsp salt

Combine yeast with other liquid ingredients, then mix with:

- 390 g AP flour

Knead until smooth and stretchy, form into a ball, and rest until
doubled in size (45-60 minutes).

Place in an airtight container in fridge overnight.

Soak overnight in flavorful liquid of your choice:

- 1/3 cup currants or dark raisins
- 1/3 cup golden raisins
- 1/3 cup dried cranberries

Drain and mix with:

- 1 tsp cinnamon
- 1/2 tsp mace
- 1/2 tsp nutmeg
- 1/2 tsp allspice
- grated zest of 1 orange
- grated zest of 1 lemon

Remove dough from fridge and allow to come up to room temp for 30 minutes.

Knead fruit and spices into dough. Form into a tight ball. Split into
quarters and form into balls, then split each ball into thirds to form
a total of 12 buns. Place on a sheet tray lined with baking paper and
allow to proof for 1 hour.

Prepare egg wash and brush on buns:

- 1 egg
- 2 tbsp milk

Bake in a 350F oven for 25-30 minutes.

Dip in lemon glaze:

- 1/2 cup icing sugar
- 1 tbsp lemon juice
- grated zest of 1 lemon

Mix up fondant icing and pipe crosses:

- 50 g fondant icing sugar
- 2 tsp water

Notes:

- I don't know if I mis-measured the flour, but I had to add an extra
  90 g to get a workable dough.
- I couldn't find golden raisins so I used sultanas.
- I soaked the fruit in leftover pomegranate juice.
- Lemon glaze is a bit thick. Use twice as much lemon juice next time.
- I think I accidentally added a tbsp of water to the fondant. I added
  probably another 50 g of icing sugar and ended up with a thick but
  pipeable icing that worked perfectly for the crosses.
