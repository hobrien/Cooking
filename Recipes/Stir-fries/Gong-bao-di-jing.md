# Gong Bao Di Jing

Adapted from [Chinese Cooking Demystified](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yt-pyFj2t2g)

Combine thoroughly in a medium bowl:

- one egg white
- 2 tsp corn starch

Stir in:

- 2 tsp liaojiu
- 1 tsp dark soy sauce
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 tsp sugar

Debone and cut into 1 cm cubes:

- 600 g chicken thigh

Place chicken in a bowl with marinade and combine thoroughly, then place in fridge.

Prepare sauce by combining in a small bowl:

- 4 tbsp sugar
- 4 tbsp dark Chinese vinegar
- 1 tbsp liaojiu
- 1 tsp light soy sauce
- 1/2 tsp dark soy sauce
- 1/2 tsp salt
- 1/4 tsp chicken bouillon powder

Combine in a small bowl:

- 30 Sichuan peppercorns, seeds removed
- 20 dried chilis, deseeded and cut into 1 cm pieces

Combine in a small bowl:

- 5 cm ginger, peeled and sliced
- 4 cloves garlic, sliced

Heat in wok to 150 C:

- 1–2 cups oil

Fry until light brown:

- 100 g peeled peanuts

Remove peanuts from wok and add marinated chicken. Fry stirring constantly until light brown, approx 5 minutes.

Remove chicken and all but 3 tbsp oil from wok. Fry chilis and peppercorns until fragrant, 1 min.

Add garlic and ginger and fry until fragrant, 1 min.

Add sauce around the sides of wok, heat until boiling, then add:

- 4 mini leeks, cut into 1 cm pieces

Return chicken and peanuts to wok, cook for 2–3 minutes, then serve.

## Notes

- The original recipe calls for much briefer cooking of chicken, but thighs benefit from long cooking. If using chicken breast, remove as soon as they are cooked through (30 sec or so) and add back to wok immediately before serving.
