# Pad See Ew

Adapted from The Wok.

Combine chicken marinade ingredients in a medium bowl:

- 1 tsp light soy sauce
- 1 tsp cornstarch
- 1/4 tsp baking soda
- pinch salt

Thinly slice and wash in a large bowl of water:

- 175 g chicken breast

Drain chicken and squeeze out water. Add to bowl with marinade and
combine thoroughly.

Combine sauce ingredients in a small bowl:

- 1.5 tbsp (18 g) sugar
- 1.5 tbsp (20 g) oyster sauce
- 1.5 tbsp (25 g) dark soy sauce
- 1.5 tbsp (25 g) light soy sauce

Mince and place in small bowl:

- 4 medium garlic cloves

Blanch in boiling water for 2 minutes, transfer to bowl of cold water
for 5 minutes, then drain:

- 6 oz purple sprouting broccoli, cut into bite-sized pieces

Place in boiling water and cook 4–5 minutes:

- 280 g thick rice noodles

Drain noodles and place on bamboo tray. Hold fan in front of noodles
and lift with chopsticks to cool. Once cooled, place in cold wok and
toss with 1 tbsp oil, then transfer to baking sheet.

Heat wok to smoking and add oil, then chicken. Spread out in a single
layer and cook 2 minutes, then stir-fry until cooked through and
return to bowl.

Add oil to empty wok and add:

- 1 egg

Cook until brown on bottom, then flip.

Add garlic and break up egg.

Add noodles and toss so egg is mostly on top. Cook for 2–3 minutes.

Add sauce and toss to coat. Cook for one minute. Toss and cook an
additional minute.

Add chicken and broccoli. Stir-fry until everything is hot, using
torch to char broccoli and noodles.

Season with:

- salt
- white pepper
- light soy sauce

Serve with:

- 3 Thai chilies, chopped
- 1/4 cup rice vinegar

## Notes

- This is 50% more sauce than the original recipe calls for. The
  noodles were a bit under-sauced when I made it, possibly because I
  used more noodles than the recipe calls for (280 g dried vs 280 g
  fresh).
- The original recipe suggests a couple more involved condiments, but
  I'm usually too lazy to do more than chilies in vinegar.
